One Call, Framing Through Finish: How It Works

A behind-the-scenes look at how our five companies coordinate to deliver seamless, integrated construction.

Quick Answer

The "one call, framing through finish" model means a general contractor makes a single phone call to engage up to five specialized trade companies — framing, drywall, interiors, exteriors, and development — all operating under the US Construction umbrella. Each company maintains its own specialized crews and expertise, but they share scheduling systems, quality standards, and project coordination, eliminating the transition delays and communication gaps that plague traditionally-subbed projects.

How the Process Works

When a GC calls US Construction, the process starts with a single bid package. Our estimating team evaluates the project across all applicable trades and returns one consolidated proposal. The GC gets a single contract, a single point of contact, and a single schedule that covers every phase we are handling.

Step 1: Unified Preconstruction

Before the first nail is driven, our preconstruction team reviews the plans across all trades simultaneously. US Framing identifies structural requirements. US Drywall reviews wall assemblies and fire-rating needs. US Exteriors evaluates the building envelope. US Interiors plans finishing sequences. This cross-trade review catches coordination issues before they become costly change orders.

Step 2: Integrated Scheduling

Our project managers build a single master schedule that maps every trade transition. When US Framing completes a floor, US Drywall is already mobilized and ready to start the next day — not next week. When drywall is finished, US Interiors has their trim crews lined up. There are no gaps because the same family controls the sequence.

Step 3: Coordinated Execution

During construction, our site superintendents coordinate across all trades daily. Morning huddles include representatives from every active US company on site. Issues are identified and resolved in real-time, without the back-and-forth emails and blame-shifting that happen when independent subs are involved.

Step 4: Single Punch List

At project completion, we conduct a single punch list walk that covers every trade. Instead of coordinating five separate sub-final inspections with five different companies, the GC walks with one US Construction representative who owns every item on the list. Items are resolved faster because the fix does not require coordinating between independent entities.

Real Results

On our Summit Student Living project in Lexington, KY, the one-call model delivered measurable results: 3 weeks ahead of schedule, 12% cost savings versus traditional bidding, and zero coordination-related change orders. The developer told us the schedule acceleration alone saved over $200,000 in carrying costs.

You Do Not Have to Use All Five

The beauty of our model is flexibility. GCs can engage one company for a specialized scope or bundle multiple companies for integrated delivery. Need just framing and drywall? Done. Need the full framing-through-finish package managed by US Development? We do that too. The integrated pricing advantage scales with the number of trades you bundle.
